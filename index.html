About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the School of Computing, Queen's University, Canada, working under the supervision of Dr. Mohammad Zulkernine (CRC Tier 1) and Dr. Jianbing Ni. I also served as an Adjunct Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Queen's University in Fall 2024. My research focuses on Generative AI Security, Digital Forensics, Trustworthy LLMs, and Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Guelph (2021-2024) under the supervision of the late Prof. Xiaodong Lin (IEEE Fellow), where I specialized in audio forensic analysis and authentication using deep learning. Prior to that, I earned my M.Sc. in Cyber Security from Xidian University, China.

My work has been recognized with two Best Paper Awards (PST 2024 and ISC 2021), and I have published in top-tier venues including USENIX Security, IEEE TDSC, IEEE TMM, IEEE TIFS, IEEE IoT Journal, ESORICS, DFRWS, and ICASSP.

Research Projects

Watermarking and Provenance for Generative AI

Jul 2024 – Present

Focus: LLMs, Text-to-Speech, Text-to-Image Generation

  • Developed robust, quality-preserving watermarking for AI-synthesized speech to support deepfake detection and integrity verification (CANARY, IEEE TMM 2026)
  • Proposed Stratified Count Fairness (SCF) to resolve the Global-Local mismatch in PRF-based red/green LLM watermarking under head-limited decoding
  • Established information-theoretic impossibility results for token-level watermark recovery in multi-LLM pipelines
  • Investigated model-agnostic watermark security for text-to-image generation, including MarkNull (USENIX Security 2026), MarkSweep (ICASSP 2026), and SafeMark
  • Designed SkillShield, a prompt-space security framework for LLM coding agents
  • Studied jailbreak resilience of GPT/DeepSeek and proposed SafeLLM for harmful output unlearning

Attacks and Defenses for Deep Recognition

Jul 2024 – Present

Focus: Privacy-Preserving Analytics, Autonomous Vehicles

  • Developed embedding reconstruction attacks bridging black-box and no-box settings (IEEE TDSC 2026)
  • Evaluated privacy leakage risks in deep recognition systems
  • Investigated privacy-preserving data release for spatial-temporal density analytics using generative adversarial mechanisms (IEEE TIFS, minor revision; corresponding author)
  • Studied privacy-preserving unsupervised anomaly detection for connected and autonomous vehicles (PUAD-CAV)
  • Co-authored comprehensive survey on secure and robust watermarking for AI-generated images

Audio Forensics and Authentication

Jan 2021 – Jun 2024

Focus: Deepfake Detection, Speaker Authentication, Background Noise Analysis

  • Proposed proactive audio authenticity via speaker-identity watermarking (PST 2024, Best Paper Award)
  • Designed BlackFeather, a background-noise forensics framework for source/context analysis (DFRWS-USA 2022 / FSI:DI 2022)
  • Developed efficient and privacy-preserving speaker recognition protocols for cybertwin-driven 6G settings (IEEE IoT Journal 2021)
  • Built evaluation pipelines for robustness and quality trade-offs under compression, noise, and distribution shift

Biometric Security and Spoofing Attacks

Sep 2017 – Dec 2020

Focus: Voice Reconstruction, Speaker Verification, Fingerprint Authentication

  • Co-developed Voxstructor: Voice reconstruction from voiceprints (ISC 2021, Best Paper Award)
  • Developed and evaluated spoofing attacks on speaker verification systems using generated voice (ICC 2019)
  • Studied attacks and protection mechanisms for biometric template data
  • Worked on privacy-preserving verification protocols for authentication systems
