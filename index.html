About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the School of Computing, Queen's University, Canada, working under the supervision of Dr. Mohammad Zulkernine and Dr. Jianbing Ni. My research focuses on Generative AI Security, Digital Forensics, and Applied Cryptography.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Guelph (2021-2024), where I specialized in audio forensic analysis and authentication using deep learning. Prior to that, I earned my M.Sc. in Cyber Security from Xidian University, China, and my B.Sc. in Computational Mathematics from East China Jiaotong University, where I ranked 1st out of 55 students.

Research Projects

Watermarking and Provenance for Generative AI

Jul 2024 – Present

Focus: LLMs, Text-to-Speech, Text-to-Image Generation

  • Developed robust, quality-preserving watermarking for AI-synthesized speech to support deepfake detection and integrity verification
  • Proposed Stratified Count Fairness (SCF) to resolve the Global-Local mismatch in PRF-based red/green LLM watermarking under head-limited decoding
  • Designed HF/SSQ operating points for forensic vs. low-latency deployment scenarios
  • Investigated model-agnostic watermark security for text-to-image generation, including no-box/black-box removal attacks
  • Studied watermark-preserving editability of watermarked images under robustness constraints
  • Conducted security analysis of jailbreak resilience and explored unlearning harmful outputs in LLMs

Attacks and Defenses for Deep Recognition

Jul 2024 – Present

Focus: Privacy-Preserving Analytics, Autonomous Vehicles

  • Developed embedding reconstruction attacks bridging black-box and no-box settings
  • Evaluated privacy leakage risks in deep recognition systems
  • Investigated privacy-preserving data release for spatial-temporal density analytics using generative mechanisms
  • Served as corresponding author on generative privacy research
  • Studied privacy-preserving unsupervised anomaly detection for connected and autonomous vehicles
  • Co-authored comprehensive survey on secure and robust watermarking for AI-generated images

Audio Forensics and Authentication

Jan 2021 – Jun 2024

Focus: Deepfake Detection, Speaker Authentication, Background Noise Analysis

  • Proposed proactive audio authenticity via speaker-identity watermarking for integrity verification
  • Designed BlackFeather, a background-noise forensics framework for source/context analysis
  • Developed efficient and privacy-preserving speaker recognition protocols for cybertwin-driven 6G settings
  • Built evaluation pipelines for robustness and quality trade-offs under compression, noise, and distribution shift

Biometric Security and Spoofing Attacks

Sep 2017 – Dec 2020

Focus: Voice Reconstruction, Speaker Verification, Fingerprint Authentication

  • Co-developed Voxstructor: Voice reconstruction from voiceprints, analyzing inversion risks
  • Developed and evaluated spoofing attacks on speaker verification systems using generated voice
  • Studied attacks and protection mechanisms for biometric template data
  • Worked on privacy-preserving verification protocols for authentication systems
